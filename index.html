MA school districts still aren't reporting how often students are arrested

A 2018 state law required Massachusetts to start collecting and publishing data on school-based arrests of students. I found that five years later, only one in 10 districts and charter schools have reported arrests. Advocates say the data is sorely needed to assess the impact of policing in schools.

By MARINA VILLENEUVE

Published August 10, 2023, 9 a.m. EST

The 2018 Criminal Justice Reform Act required superintendents and police chiefs in Massachusetts to enter into written agreements to specify who's responsible for collecting and reporting school-based arrests, citations and court referrals to the state

Data collection started in the 2018-2019 school year, and the first year of reporting was spotty.

But years later, concerns raised by advocate group Citizens for Juvenile Justice are still lingering as the latest state data shows a continued troubling lack of data reported by well over 300 Massachusetts school districts and charter schools.
